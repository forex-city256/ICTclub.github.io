About ICT Club Vision

What is an ICT Club?

An ICT Club is a student organization that promotes digital literacy, coding skills, and technological innovation. Our club provides a platform for students to explore computer science, programming, robotics, and digital media in a collaborative environment.

We aim to bridge the digital divide in African schools by providing access to technology and training that might not be available in standard curriculum. Our mission is to create a generation of digitally empowered African youth who can leverage technology to solve local challenges and compete globally.

Since our establishment in 2023, we have trained over 20 students in various digital skills, participated in 3 competitions so far, and launched 3 community tech projects.

Facilities & Equipment

Our ICT club is equipped with modern technology to facilitate learning and innovation:

  • 30 high-performance computers with high-speed internet access
  • 3D printer for prototyping and design projects
  • Arduino and Raspberry Pi kits for electronics and IoT projects
  • Programming kits for hands-on learning and competitions
  • Graphic design software (Adobe Creative Suite) and digital media tools
  • Video conferencing equipment for virtual collaborations with other African schools
  • VR headset for immersive learning experiences

Our facilities are open Monday to Saturday for club members to work on projects, practice skills, and collaborate with peers.

Club Terms & Conditions

By joining ICT Club Vision for Africa, members agree to adhere to the following terms and conditions that ensure a productive, safe, and inclusive environment for all.

1. Membership Eligibility

Open to all students from Form 1 to Form 6. No prior ICT experience required. Members must maintain good academic standing and follow all school rules. Membership renewal is required at the beginning of each academic year.

2. Code of Conduct

All members must adhere to the following:

  • Use club equipment responsibly, ethically, and only for educational purposes
  • Respect other members, teachers, and guest speakers regardless of skill level
  • Adhere to copyright and intellectual property laws in all projects
  • Maintain a positive, inclusive, and collaborative learning environment
  • Report any equipment issues or safety concerns immediately to club leadership or teachers
  • No bullying, discrimination, or harassment of any form will be tolerated

3. Attendance Policy

Members are expected to attend at least 60% of scheduled meetings and activities. Excessive absences without valid reason may result in suspension of equipment borrowing privileges. Members must sign in at each meeting for attendance records.

4. Equipment Use & Responsibility

Club equipment is for educational purposes only during club hours or with special permission. Members are financially responsible for any damage caused by negligence or misuse. All equipment must be signed out and returned in the same condition. Food and drinks are strictly prohibited near computers and electronic equipment.

5. Data Privacy & Online Safety

Member information will be kept confidential and used only for club purposes. Photos and videos may be taken during club activities for promotional materials with consent. Members must practice safe online behavior and protect personal information. Cyberbullying or inappropriate online behavior will result in immediate expulsion.

6. Safety Guidelines

All safety protocols must be followed, especially when working with electronics, soldering equipment, or power tools. Emergency procedures are posted in the club room and must be reviewed annually. First aid kit is available in the club room for minor injuries. Serious incidents must be reported immediately to club teachers.

7. Project Ownership & Intellectual Property

Projects created using club resources belong to both the creator and the club. The club reserves the right to showcase projects for promotional purposes. Members must give credit to all contributors in group projects. Commercial use of projects developed with club resources requires club approval.

8. Disciplinary Action

Violation of club rules may result in:

  • Verbal warning and counseling
  • Written warning with parent/guardian notification
  • Temporary suspension from club activities (1-4 weeks)
  • Permanent expulsion from the club
  • Financial responsibility for damages or losses caused
  • Reporting to school administration for serious violations

9. Amendments to Terms

These terms may be amended by the club leadership with approval from teacher advisors and majority vote from active members. Members will be notified of any changes at least one week before they take effect. Current terms are always available on the club website and notice board.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Who can join the ICT Club?

A: Any student from Form 1 to Form 6, regardless of prior ICT experience.

Q: Is there a membership fee?

A: Yes, membership is 5k for all students. Some activities like tours may have minimal costs.

Q: When are new members accepted?

A: We accept new members at the beginning of each term. Orientation sessions are held in September, January, and April.

Q: Can I borrow club equipment?

A: Yes, active members can borrow certain equipment for projects with permission from club teachers.
